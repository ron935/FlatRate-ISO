Why Government?

The Most Stable Residuals
in Payments

Most payment agents chase restaurants and retail — which have high turnover and frequent processor-switching. Government entities are the opposite. Once you win a municipality, that account processes for years, sometimes decades. The contract doesn't change. The volume doesn't leave. And you keep getting paid.

Governments Don't Switch Processors

Procurement cycles are long and painful. Once a vendor is approved, municipalities rarely change unless something goes dramatically wrong. Your residual is protected by bureaucracy.

Massive, Predictable Volume

Utility payments, permit fees, court fines, tax payments, recreation registrations — government entities process enormous volume every single month, like clockwork.

Sales Playbook

How to Sell to Government

Government is different from retail. The decision-making process is longer — but the payoff is dramatically more stable. Here's what works.

Step 1: Identify Your Target

Start with cities and counties in your territory with populations between 5,000–100,000. Mid-size municipalities are the sweet spot — large enough for meaningful volume, small enough that you can reach the Finance Director directly.

Step 2: Lead with Government Portal

Don't pitch "merchant services." Introduce Government Portal as a resident payment platform. The conversation shifts from cost to capability — and that's where you win government accounts.

Step 3: Navigate Procurement

FlatRate ISO provides compliance documentation, RFP response templates, and reference municipalities. We've been through this process hundreds of times and have materials ready for every stage.

Step 4: Close & Collect for Years

Government contracts are typically 3–5 years. Once signed, your residual from that single account can represent $400–$1,200/month in passive income for the entire contract term — with renewal built in.
